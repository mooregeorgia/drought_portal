Vulnerability Index:

Index (DRI) = SUM(Indices)/Count(Indices)
  • A high score indicates a high vulnerability risk
  • A low score indicates a low vulnerability risk

Normalisation:

zi = (xi−min(x))/(max(x)−min(x))

Education and Occupation:

The SEIFA Index of Education and Occupation (IEO) is designed to reflect the educational and occupational level of communities.

  • A high score indicates relatively lower education and occupation status of people in the area in general.
  • A low score indicates relatively higher education and occupation status of people in the area in general.

Economic Resources:

The Index of Economic Resources (IER) focuses on the financial aspects of relative socio-economic advantage and disadvantage, by summarising variables related to income and wealth.

  • A high score indicates a relative lack of access to economic resources in general.
  • A low score indicates relatively greater access to economic resources in general.

Social Dependency:

Percentage of LGA population aged between 15 and 65 years old.

  • A high score indicates a high vulnerability risk as the working age population is high.
  • A low score indicates a low vulnerability risk as the working age population is low.
